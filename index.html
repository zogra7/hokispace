A brief narrative on building things

I spend my time at the intersection of writing code and trying to make sense of the world. For me, building software is not about chasing fleeting tech trends or creating platforms filled with corporate jargon. It's about solving real-world problems, creating layouts that are actually accessible, and finding joy in the craft of coding.

Most of my growth occurs when things break. I've spent days debugging alignment glitches, and each mistake has made me a better builder. I value clean execution, honest layouts, and direct communication.

When my screen is off, I'm usually reading, studying editorial layouts, or trying to construct something physical. I believe that consistency, continuous small improvements, and genuine curiosity are the only ways to build a meaningful life.
